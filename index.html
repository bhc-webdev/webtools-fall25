Getting Started at Launch Studio!

My Launch Studio Introduction

About Me

My name is Stephen Herd, and I am beginning my journey into web development with Launch Studio. I've always been curious about how websites are built and how technology connects people, but until now, most of my experience has been on the user side of the screen. This class gives me the chance to move from being a consumer of the web to becoming a creator who understands the structure and logic behind the sites I use every day.

Outside of school, I have a variety of interests that shape who I am as a person. I enjoy reading, gaming, fitness, music, or volunteering, and I often find connections between those hobbies and the skills I want to develop as a coder. For example, gaming has made me curious about interactive design.

One of my goals as a student is to grow not just technically but also creatively. I want to learn how to take an idea and bring it to life on the web, while also developing habits that make me a reliable collaborator. By the end of this course, I hope to feel confident introducing myself as someone who doesn't just study web development but actively practices it.

Developer Identity

When I think about my developer identity, I see myself as someone who is just starting to learn the language of the web but already beginning to picture the kind of professional I want to become. At this stage, I identify most as a “junior developer in training.” I don't yet have all the answers, but I am learning how to ask the right questions, test my ideas, and rely on structured problem-solving to move forward.

My strengths as a developer come from qualities I already value in other areas of my life: persistence, curiosity, and creativity. I may not write perfect code on the first try, but I am willing to debug, research, and rework my approach until I find a solution. I also enjoy looking for patterns, whether in code, design, or workflows, because patterns help me understand how complex systems can be broken down into manageable steps.

Over time, I want my developer identity to reflect confidence and professionalism. I want to be seen not just as a student who completes assignments but as someone who brings a unique voice to projects and collaborates well with others. For now, I am comfortable identifying as a developer-in-progress who is building both technical skills and professional habits that will carry me into future opportunities.

Learning Journey

My decision to take this course comes from a desire to better understand how the web works and to gain skills that are practical, creative, and in demand. I have used websites for as long as I can remember, but I've often wondered what happens behind the scenes. This course marks the beginning of my journey to move from curiosity into capability, where I can start building and not just browsing.

I expect that this journey will not always be easy. I know there will be moments of frustration when code doesn't work or when I feel stuck on a problem. But I also see those challenges as part of the process. Each mistake is a chance to learn, and each small success is a step forward. I want to develop resilience as much as I develop technical knowledge, because both are essential to becoming a capable web developer.

Looking ahead, I hope to use the skills from this class to create projects that reflect both my personality and my professional goals. I would like to design pages that are clean, accessible, and meaningful for real users. Beyond this class, I want to keep expanding my knowledge into areas like responsive design, interactivity, and eventually full-stack development. This course is the launchpad for that larger journey, and I am excited to take the first steps here.
